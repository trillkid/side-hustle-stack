# Pinterest Strategy — 10 Ready-to-Post Pins

Pinterest is a goldmine for product reviews. People go there specifically to research purchases. Unlike Twitter (where posts die in hours), Pinterest pins can drive traffic for YEARS. Here are 10 ready-to-post pins.

---

## How Pinterest works for affiliate marketing

1. People search Pinterest for "best headphones" or "home office setup"
2. Your pin shows up with a photo + title
3. They click → land on your article → click your Amazon link → you earn

**Key:** Pinterest wants VERTICAL images (2:3 ratio, like 1000x1500px) and keyword-rich descriptions.

---

## How to create the pin images (free)

Use **Canva.com** (free):
1. Sign up → search "Pinterest pin" templates
2. Pick a template → change the text to match your pin title below
3. Use a product photo from Unsplash (free) or Amazon product images
4. Download as PNG
5. Upload to Pinterest with the title + description + your article URL

## Pinterest posting strategy

### 1. Create a free Pinterest business account
- Go to **business.pinterest.com** → sign up
- Use your site URL: `https://side-hustle-stack-beta.vercel.app/`
- Claim your website (Pinterest gives you a code to add — I can help with this)

### 2. Create boards
Make boards with keyword-rich names:
- "Tech Reviews 2025"
- "Desk Setup Inspiration"
- "Work From Home Gear"
- "Travel Tech Essentials"
- "Gift Guides"

### 3. Pin all 10 pins
- Upload each pin image → add the title + description → link to your site
- Pin each one to the most relevant board
- Space them out — 1-2 pins per day, not all at once

### 4. Pin consistently
Pinterest rewards consistency. Aim for:
- 1-2 new pins per day
- Re-pin other people's content to your boards (Pinterest likes active pinners)
- Fresh pins (new images) perform better than re-pins

### 5. Use Pinterest SEO
- Pinterest is a SEARCH ENGINE, not social media
- Use keywords in pin titles, descriptions, and board names
- Think: "What would someone search to find this?" → use those exact words

### 6. Patience
Pinterest is SLOW. Pins take 3-6 months to start getting traffic. But once they do, they can drive clicks for years. Do not quit after a week.

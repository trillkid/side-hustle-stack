# The Logitech MX Master 3S Made Me a Faster Worker — Here's How

**Tags: productivity, tech review, work from home, mouse, logitech**

I didn't think a mouse could meaningfully change how fast I work. I was wrong.

After three months of daily use, the Logitech MX Master 3S has wormed its way so deeply into my workflow that when I recently had to use a regular mouse for a day, I felt like I was working in slow motion. Here's what makes this mouse special — and whether it's worth the $99.

## The scroll wheel is the whole game-changer

This is the feature that sells the mouse, and it lives up to the hype. The MagSpeed wheel has two modes:

**Ratchet mode:** You feel each notch as you scroll — precise, controlled, great for reading documents line by line.

**Free-spin mode:** Flick the wheel and it spins freely for what feels like forever. I can scroll from the top of a 200-page PDF to the bottom in one motion. No exaggeration.

It switches between modes automatically based on how fast you flick, or you can press a button to toggle manually. I use free-spin constantly for long documents, spreadsheets, and code files. It sounds minor, but it saves me dozens of micro-scrolls per day.

## Silent clicks that don't feel mushy

The "S" in MX Master 3S means silent clicks. I was skeptical — silent mice usually feel mushy and unsatisfying. Logitech somehow made the clicks quiet AND tactile. You still feel a definite click, but it's maybe 30% as loud as a normal mouse.

If you work in a shared office, on calls, or late at night when others are sleeping, this is genuinely valuable. I can click away during a Zoom meeting without it sounding like I'm typing aggressively.

## The shape: comfortable for hours

The MX Master 3S is shaped for a right-handed palm grip. It's ergonomic in a way that feels natural immediately — no adjustment period. I have medium-sized hands, and every button falls right where my fingers rest.

I've done 8-hour workdays with this mouse and never had hand cramps. The thumb rest on the side is a small detail that makes a big difference over long sessions.

**Left-handed users:** sorry, this is righties only. Logitech doesn't make a left-handed version.

## Two hidden features I now can't live without

### 1. The thumb wheel (horizontal scroll)
On the thumb rest, there's a second wheel that scrolls horizontally. I use it constantly in Excel, Figma, and wide code files. Once you have horizontal scroll on a mouse, you miss it everywhere else.

### 2. Flow (use one mouse across two computers)
This is wild. Logitech's Flow software lets you control two computers with one mouse — drag your cursor to the edge of screen 1 and it appears on screen 2. You can even copy text on one computer and paste it on the other.

I use this with my laptop and desktop. It's like having a KVM switch without the hardware. If you work across two machines, this alone justifies the price.

## The not-so-great stuff

**It's heavy.** At 141g, this is not a gaming mouse. If you play fast-paced shooters, look elsewhere. For productivity work, the weight actually feels stable and premium.

**Bluetooth can be finicky on some setups.** I had occasional dropouts on one of my machines. Switching to the included USB receiver fixed it completely. The receiver tucks into the mouse for travel.

**It's not cheap.** $99 for a mouse is steep. But if you use a mouse 8 hours a day, it's one of the best dollars-per-hour-of-use investments you can make.

**No USB-C charging while using wireless.** You can use it wired (it charges via USB-C), but you can't use it wirelessly while charging. Minor annoyance. Battery lasts about 70 days per charge, so this rarely comes up.

## Battery life is excellent

Logitech claims 70 days per charge. I got about 60 days with heavy daily use. A 1-minute quick charge gives you 3 hours of use. I charge it for about 15 minutes once every two months and forget about it.

## Who should buy this

**Buy it if:**
- You work at a computer 4+ hours daily
- You scroll through long documents, spreadsheets, or code
- You use two computers and want one mouse for both
- You value build quality and ergonomics

**Skip it if:**
- You're a competitive gamer (get a lightweight gaming mouse)
- You're left-handed
- You're on a tight budget (a $20 mouse does 80% of what this does)

## The verdict

The Logitech MX Master 3S is the best productivity mouse I've used, and it's not close. The scroll wheel alone makes it worth the price for anyone who works with documents or spreadsheets daily. Add in the ergonomics, silent clicks, and Flow feature, and it's a genuine workflow upgrade.

I was skeptical that a $99 mouse could meaningfully improve my work speed. Three months later, I can't go back to a regular mouse without feeling slowed down. That's the hallmark of a tool worth owning.

**Rating: 4.7 / 5**

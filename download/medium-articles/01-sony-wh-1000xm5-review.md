# Sony WH-1000XM5 Review: I Wore These Headphones for 30 Days — Here's My Honest Verdict

**Tags: headphones, tech review, productivity, work from home, sony**

I'll cut to the chase: I've owned a lot of "premium" headphones over the years, and most of them weren't worth the premium price tag. But after wearing the Sony WH-1000XM5 every single day for a month — on calls, on flights, on noisy coffee shop afternoons — I get why people won't shut up about them.

Let me break down what's genuinely great, what's annoying, and whether you should actually drop $400 on them.

## The noise cancelling is absurd

This is the reason you buy these. Sony's adaptive noise cancellation is, as of 2025, the best you can get in a consumer headphone. I tested it in the harshest environment I could think of: a transatlantic flight in a window seat right over the wing.

With ANC on and music playing at a moderate volume, the engine roar disappeared. Not "dimmed" — gone. I could hear the person next to me if they tapped my shoulder, but the cabin noise simply wasn't there anymore. I got off the flight feeling less tired than I usually do, and I'm convinced the noise reduction is why.

For daily work-from-home use, it's equally transformative. My apartment faces a busy street, and the constant traffic hum that I'd stopped consciously noticing was suddenly absent. The XM5s create this weird, almost eerie bubble of silence that lets you actually focus.

## The sound: warm, detailed, never fatiguing

I'm not an audiophile who'll talk your ear off about "soundstage" and "timbre," but I know what sounds good. The XM5 delivers a warm, full-bodied sound that's easy to listen to for hours. Bass is present and punchy without overwhelming the mids. Vocals — podcasts, audiobooks, vocals in music — are crisp and forward.

The one criticism: out of the box, the sound is slightly bass-forward. If you prefer a flatter, more neutral profile, you'll want to tweak the EQ in Sony's app. But most people will love it as-is.

## Battery life that actually delivers

Sony claims 30 hours with ANC on. In my real-world testing — mixed use of music, calls, and ANC — I got about 27 hours per charge. That's a full week of workdays on a single charge. And a 3-minute quick charge gives you 3 hours of playback, which has saved me more than once before a flight.

## The not-so-great stuff

**They're not foldable.** The previous generation (XM4) folded into a compact bundle. The XM5s fold flat but don't collapse, so they take up more bag space. If you travel light, this matters.

**They're not water-resistant.** No IP rating. Don't wear them to the gym. Sweat will kill them.

**The price.** At $400, they're an investment. If you're not going to use them daily, it's hard to justify.

**Touch controls have a learning curve.** The right earcup is touch-sensitive — tap to play/pause, swipe to change volume. It works, but I'd accidentally trigger it leaning against a headrest. There's a physical button for ANC/ambient mode, which I used more.

## The call quality surprised me

I expected the XM5s to be mediocre for calls, like most headphones. But Sony upgraded the mic array, and the result is genuinely good call quality. People on the other end of Zoom calls said I sounded clear even when I was in a noisy café. The mics do a solid job of isolating your voice from background noise.

## Who should buy these

**Buy them if:**
- You work from home or in noisy environments
- You travel frequently (especially flying)
- You wear headphones 4+ hours a day
- You value noise cancelling above all else

**Skip them if:**
- You want gym headphones (get sweat-resistant ones instead)
- You're on a strict budget (the XM4 is still excellent and often $100 cheaper)
- You need foldable headphones for tight bag space

## The verdict

After 30 days, the Sony WH-1000XM5 is the best headphone I've owned. The noise cancelling alone justifies the price if you'll use it daily. The sound, battery, and call quality are all excellent. The lack of foldability is the only real downside, and it's a trade-off Sony made for the improved design.

If you're on the fence between these and the Bose QuietComfort Ultra, I'd give the edge to Sony for sound quality and value. Bose wins slightly on comfort, but Sony wins on almost everything else.

**Rating: 4.8 / 5**

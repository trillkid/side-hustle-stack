# I Charged My MacBook and iPhone at the Same Time With One Power Bank — The Anker 737 Review

**Tags: tech review, gadgets, travel, anker, power bank**

I bought the Anker 737 Power Bank (24,000mAh, 120W) before a two-week trip, expecting it to be "just another power bank." It's not. It's the first power bank I've used that actually charges my MacBook Pro at full speed — and it might be the only charger I travel with from now on.

Here's my honest take after 60 days of use.

## The headline: 120W changes everything

Most power banks output 15-30 watts. That's enough to slow-charge a phone. The Anker 737 outputs up to 120 watts — enough to fast-charge a MacBook Pro, a iPad Pro, and a phone, sometimes all at once.

What does this mean in practice?

- **MacBook Pro (16"):** Went from 20% to 50% in 30 minutes while I was using it. That's the same speed as the wall charger.
- **iPhone 15:** 0 to 60% in 25 minutes.
- **Two devices at once:** MacBook at 60W + iPhone at 30W simultaneously, no slowdown.

If you've ever watched your laptop battery die in an airport with no available outlet, you understand why this matters. The Anker 737 is essentially a portable wall outlet.

## Capacity: 24,000mAh is the sweet spot

This capacity gives you:
- ~1.5 full MacBook Pro charges
- ~5 full iPhone charges
- ~3 full iPad charges

It's enough for a long travel day, a camping weekend, or a power outage, without being so heavy that you don't want to carry it. At 630g (1.4 lbs), it's noticeable in a backpack but not burdensome.

**Important:** 24,000mAh is under the 27,000mAh FAA limit, so you can carry it on flights (but not check it). The Anker 737 is flight-safe — I've taken it through TSA three times with no issues.

## Three ports, no dongles needed

This is a detail I appreciate more than I expected. The Anker 737 has:
- **2 USB-C ports** (both can output up to 120W)
- **1 USB-A port** (up to 18W)

You can charge three devices simultaneously. The power distribution is smart — plug in two USB-C devices and they each get up to 60W. Plug in three and they split intelligently. I never had to think about which port to use.

The built-in cable is a nice touch — wait, no, there's no built-in cable. You use your own. But Anker includes a USB-C to USB-C cable in the box that handles the full 120W.

## The display is genuinely useful

There's a small digital display on the side that shows:
- Current output wattage per port
- Battery percentage remaining
- Estimated time to full (when charging) or time remaining (when discharging)

I didn't think I'd care about this. I was wrong. Knowing "38 minutes to full" or "this port is pulling 87W" is surprisingly handy. It confirms the charger is actually delivering the speed it promises.

## The not-so-great stuff

**It's bulky.** This isn't a pocket power bank. It's about the size of a small hardcover book. Fits fine in a backpack, but you're not putting it in a jacket pocket.

**It's heavy for its size.** 630g isn't light. If you're a minimalist traveler who counts every gram, this might be more than you need.

**It takes a while to recharge.** The power bank itself takes 3-4 hours to recharge from empty, even with a high-wattage charger. The trade-off for holding 24,000mAh. I charge it overnight.

**Pricey.** At around $130-150, it's one of the more expensive power banks. Cheaper 10,000mAh power banks exist for $25 if you just need phone charges.

## Build quality: it feels like a tank

Anker makes solid gear, and the 737 feels premium. Metal casing, no creaks, ports that hold cables firmly. After 60 days of being tossed in backpacks and dropped on desks, mine has zero scratches or issues. The finish still looks new.

## Who should buy this

**Buy it if:**
- You travel with a laptop and want to charge it away from outlets
- You're a photographer/videographer who charges gear in the field
- You go to conferences or coworking spaces where outlets are scarce
- You want one power bank that handles everything

**Skip it if:**
- You only need to charge your phone (a $25 10,000mAh bank is fine)
- You want something pocket-sized
- You're on a tight budget

## The verdict

The Anker 737 is the power bank I didn't know I needed. After two months, it's become essential travel gear — the thing I grab before my laptop charger because it can do everything the wall charger can, anywhere.

At $130-150, it's not cheap. But if you travel with a laptop, work remotely, or just hate being tethered to wall outlets, it pays for itself in convenience within weeks. I've used it on flights, in airports, in parks, and during a 6-hour power outage. Every time, it delivered exactly what it promised.

If Anker made a smaller, 10,000mAh version with 65W output, I'd buy that too for light days. But for full-day travel, the 737 is the one.

**Rating: 4.6 / 5**
